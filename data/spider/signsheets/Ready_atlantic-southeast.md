### The Oldest City
- treatment: billboard
- leg: jacksonville_fl_us -> miami_fl_us
- at_mi: 40
- spoken: Billboard: Saint Augustine, the oldest city in America. Founded by the Spanish in fifteen sixty-five, more than forty years before Jamestown. There is a stone fort that never fell, a college in a palace, and a spring that Ponce de Leon called the Fountain of Youth.
- describe: Saint Augustine, founded in fifteen sixty-five, is the oldest continuously occupied European-established settlement in the continental United States. It holds the Castillo de San Marcos, the oldest masonry fort in the country, Flagler College in a former grand hotel, and the tourist Fountain of Youth tied to Ponce de Leon.

### The Golden Isles
- treatment: billboard
- leg: jacksonville_fl_us -> brunswick_ga_us
- at_mi: 66
- spoken: Billboard: Brunswick and the Golden Isles. Jekyll Island, where the richest men in America built a winter club and quietly drew up the Federal Reserve. Saint Simons and its marshes, the marshes of Glynn a poet made famous. And Brunswick stew, which Brunswick swears it invented.
- describe: Brunswick is the gateway to Georgia's Golden Isles, including Jekyll Island, whose exclusive club hosted the secret nineteen ten meeting that shaped the Federal Reserve, and Saint Simons Island. The surrounding salt marshes inspired Sidney Lanier's poem The Marshes of Glynn. Brunswick claims to have invented Brunswick stew.

### The City of Squares
- treatment: billboard
- leg: charleston_sc_us -> savannah_ga_us
- at_mi: 105
- spoken: Billboard: Savannah, the city of squares, hung with Spanish moss. Its oldest square is where Forrest Gump sat with his box of chocolates. Cobblestones, ghost tours, an art school in every old building, and the whole downtown a National Historic Landmark.
- describe: Savannah, laid out in eighteenth-century squares beneath live oaks and Spanish moss, is one of America's largest historic districts. Forrest Gump's bench scenes were filmed in Chippewa Square. The book Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil made its cemeteries and mansions famous; SCAD, the art college, has restored much of downtown.

### The Lowcountry
- treatment: landmark
- leg: charleston_sc_us -> savannah_ga_us
- at_mi: 50
- spoken: Crossing the Lowcountry of salt marsh and sea island, home of the Gullah Geechee people and their language, of shrimp and grits, and of the Marine boot camp at Parris Island where recruits are made or broken.
- describe: The South Carolina Lowcountry, around Beaufort and the sea islands, is the heart of Gullah Geechee culture, descended from enslaved West Africans who kept a distinct creole language and cuisine. Parris Island is the storied Marine Corps recruit depot. Beaufort's antebellum streets have starred in many films.

### Where the War Began
- treatment: billboard
- leg: columbia_sc_us -> charleston_sc_us
- at_mi: 113
- spoken: Billboard: Charleston, the Holy City of steeples, where the Civil War began with the first shot fired on Fort Sumter out in the harbor. Walk the Battery past the pastel mansions of Rainbow Row, and order the she-crab soup.
- describe: Charleston is where the Civil War opened, with Confederate guns firing on the federal garrison at Fort Sumter in April eighteen sixty-one. Nicknamed the Holy City for its many church spires, it charms visitors with the waterfront Battery, the pastel row houses of Rainbow Row, horse carriages, and Lowcountry cooking like she-crab soup.

### The Angel Oak

- at_mi: 3
- spoken: Leaving Charleston past the Angel Oak, a live oak said to be four or five centuries old, its enormous limbs drooping to the ground and rising again like a small forest from a single tree.
- describe: The Angel Oak on Johns Island near Charleston is a Southern live oak estimated to be several hundred years old, with a canopy spreading over seventeen thousand square feet and massive limbs that rest on the earth. It is one of the most photographed trees in the South.

### Famously Hot
- treatment: landmark
- leg: columbia_sc_us -> charlotte_nc_us
- at_mi: 3
- spoken: Leaving Columbia, the South Carolina capital that calls itself Famously Hot, home of the Gamecocks, and gateway to Congaree, a national park of the tallest hardwood forest left in the East, where the fireflies flash all at once.
- describe: Columbia, the capital of South Carolina, embraces the slogan Famously Hot for its summers and its music.
