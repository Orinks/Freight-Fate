### Gettysburg
- treatment: landmark
- leg: washington_dc_us -> carlisle_pa_us
- at_mi: 60
- spoken: Passing Gettysburg, the small Pennsylvania town where the greatest battle of the Civil War was fought over three July days, and where Lincoln, months later, spoke for two minutes and defined the nation.
- describe: Gettysburg saw the war's bloodiest battle and its turning point in July eighteen sixty-three. That November, at the dedication of the cemetery, Abraham Lincoln delivered the Gettysburg Address, two hundred and seventy-two words that reframed the meaning of the country.

### Amish Country
- treatment: billboard
- leg: harrisburg_pa_us -> wilmington_de_us
- at_mi: 40
- spoken: Billboard: Lancaster County. Amish country, where horse-drawn buggies share the road, the barns are hand-raised, and the whoopie pies and shoofly pie are worth the stop. Slow down for the buggies.
- describe: Lancaster County is the heart of Pennsylvania Dutch country, home to the Amish and Mennonites who farm without electricity and travel by horse and buggy. Roadside stands sell whoopie pies, shoofly pie, and quilts; the highways warn drivers to yield to the buggies.

### Pizza and Yale

- leg: new_london_ct_us -> new_haven_ct_us
- at_mi: 44
- spoken: Billboard: New Haven ahead. Home of Yale, and of a fierce claim to have invented both the hamburger and American apizza. The coal-fired white clam pie is not up for debate.

### The First Nuclear Submarine
- treatment: billboard
- leg: providence_ri_us -> new_london_ct_us
- at_mi: 60
- spoken: Billboard: Groton, the submarine capital, across the river from New London. The Nautilus, the first nuclear submarine and the first vessel to reach the North Pole under the ice, is tied up here for you to walk through.
- describe: Groton, opposite New London, is the home of the submarine force and the Electric Boat shipyard. The USS Nautilus, the world's first nuclear-powered submarine and the first to cross beneath the North Pole, is preserved here as a museum you can board. Nearby, Mystic Seaport and the pizzeria from Mystic Pizza.

### Cooperstown
- treatment: billboard
- leg: binghamton_ny_us -> utica_ny_us
- at_mi: 60
- spoken: Billboard: Turn off for Cooperstown and the Baseball Hall of Fame, a village on a lake where the game was supposedly born and where its immortals are enshrined in bronze.
- describe: Cooperstown, on Otsego Lake, is home to the National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum. By legend, Abner Doubleday invented baseball here; the legend is shaky but the shrine is real, and every summer the new inductees draw pilgrims to the tiny town.

### The Bennington Monument

- leg: albany_ny_us -> montpelier_vt_us
- at_mi: 38
- spoken: At Bennington rises the tallest thing in Vermont, a stone battle monument three hundred feet high, near the churchyard where the poet Robert Frost is buried.

### The Smallest Capital
- treatment: landmark
- leg: albany_ny_us -> montpelier_vt_us
- at_mi: 156
- spoken: Reaching Montpelier, the smallest state capital in the nation, and the only one without a single fast-food chain restaurant. A gold dome in a town of ten thousand.
- describe: Montpelier, Vermont, is the least populous state capital in the country and, famously, the only one with no McDonald's within its limits, a point of local pride. Its gold-domed statehouse sits against the green hills.
