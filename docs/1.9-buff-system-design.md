# 1.9 buff system — design draft (workshopping)

Status: DRAFT for owner workshopping, 2026-07-12. Numbers are first-pass
vibes, not balance. Decisions marked LOCKED came from the owner in chat;
everything else is open.

## Core rules (LOCKED)

- Buffs touch **fatigue and rig wear only — never the HOS duty clock**.
  The legal clock is sacred; no consumable buys back legal hours.
- **Rate modifiers are the core**: a buff slows how fast fatigue or wear
  accrues for a while. Food and drink items also give a **small instant
  fatigue kick** so a meal feels like a meal.
- **Two duration classes**: food/drink last fixed game-hours (felt
  wearing off mid-leg is the gameplay); rig services (quick lube) last
  rest-of-trip.
- **One active buff per axis, newest replaces** — a meal buff (fatigue)
  and a quick lube (engine) coexist, but three coffees don't stack; a
  new purchase on the same axis restarts/replaces. Keeps the spoken
  status one clean line per axis.
- Purchases cost money and a few minutes of game clock (deadline keeps
  counting), mirroring the existing food-break flow. Deterministic, no
  RNG in effects.
- Availability is brand/stop-type keyed via `amenities.classify_brand`
  and the stop's listed services — same rail as the service menus.
  Big Buck's items are gated by the bobtail rule (later: Golden
  Antler pass lifts the gate per visit).

Principles embedded above:

  beats the generic diner; Speedco's lube beats the additive).
- Big Buck's is the best fatigue item in the game — that's what makes
  the ban gag and the Golden Antler worth anything.
- The free-shower-with-fuel tie-in teaches a real trucking fact.

## Data architecture

- **Catalog**: `src/freight_fate/data/buffs.json` — one entry per buff:
  `id`, `label` (menu), `spoken` (effect explained in player language),
  `axis` effects (`fatigue_instant`, `fatigue_rate`, `engine_wear_rate`,
  `tire_wear_rate`), `duration` (`{"game_hours": 4}` or `"trip"`),
  `price`, `stop_minutes`, `availability` (brand keys and/or stop
  service keys, e.g. `{"brands": ["petro"]}` /
  `{"services": ["food"]}`), optional `free_with_fuel_brands`.
- **Loader**: `src/freight_fate/data/buffs.py` — parses, validates
  (unknown axis = load error), exposes `buffs_for_stop(name, services)`.
- **Profile**: `active_buffs: list[dict]` — `{id, axis multipliers,
  expires_game_h | "trip"}`. Old saves load with `[]`. Trip-scoped
  buffs are cleared at settlement; timed ones expire off the game
  clock and announce it once ("The big meal has worn off.").
- **Physics hooks**: fatigue accrual and `_update_wear` each multiply by
  the active buff's rate for that axis (default 1.0). Buffs never touch
  `hos` anywhere — enforce with a test.
- **Spoken status**: driving status + truck status read active buffs,
  one line per axis ("Meal buff: two hours left. Quick lube: rest of
  trip.").
- Mini-games and future systems grant buffs **by id** from the same
  catalog (a trivia win hands out `energy_drink` or a Golden Antler
  pass) — no parallel reward system.
