# Field recording shot list: one truck, one afternoon

What to capture when a real driver offers their truck, so no yes gets
wasted. Ordered so the most game-critical sounds come first -- if the
session gets cut short, the top of the list still pays for the trip.

Gear notes: a phone in a stable mount is acceptable for reference; a
32-bit float handheld recorder (Zoom F2/H1essential class) removes all
gain worry. Wind muff outdoors, always. For every moving shot, record
BOTH perspectives when possible: in-cab (doors closed, driver position)
and trackside (fixed mic, truck passes). Ask the driver to narrate
nothing -- clean takes, talk between them.

## Priority one: the engine's voice (feeds the RPM mapping)

1. Cold start, from key turn through settle to idle. In-cab and
   outside-hood versions.
2. Idle, two full minutes, in-cab. This is the bed everything sits on.
3. Stationary rev sweep: slow smooth rise from idle to governed max
   and back down, twice. The single most useful take in the session --
   it becomes the pitch/load map.
4. Launch through the low gears, moderate throttle, in-cab: first
   through fifth or sixth, natural shifts. This is the exact
   "click-click-click" gap: revs building, falling on the shift,
   catching again.
5. Same launch, trackside.
6. Constant-speed cruise beds, in-cab: roughly 30, 45, and 60 mph,
   one minute each, steady state.

## Priority two: the jake (the known library gap)

7. Jake brake engaging on a downgrade at high RPM, in-cab: the full
   bark from engagement to release. As many grades/stages as the truck
   and route allow (stage 1 vs 3 sound different).
8. Jake trackside if any overpass or pull-off allows it -- the
   classic neighborhood-sign sound.
9. If the session is parking-lot only: jake blips in neutral are
   better than nothing; note RPM if visible.

## Priority three: air, brakes, and the driveline

10. Air dryer purge (the sharp psshht at the fuel island).
11. Parking brake set and release, in-cab: the push-pull valves, the
    spring brake clunk.
12. Service brake applications at low speed: gentle stop, firm stop.
13. Gear engagement clunk from neutral into first, and into reverse,
    engine idling -- isolated as possible. This is the shift-sound
    asset.
14. Trailer coupling: fifth wheel strike, kingpin lock, landing gear
    crank, glad-hands connect, electrical plug.
15. Doors: cab door open/close from inside and outside, sleeper
    curtain if any.

## Priority four: texture and place

16. Reefer unit running, if the trailer has one: near and from the
    cab.
17. Walkaround at idle: mic moving slowly around the whole rig, one
    continuous take -- gives us distance/angle coloration for the
    positional traffic work.
18. Tire/road noise bed: in-cab at cruise with engine masked as much
    as possible (coasting in neutral on a quiet road is the dream
    take).
19. Rumble strip hits and a gravel shoulder excursion IF the driver
    offers -- never ask for anything off-nominal.
20. Truck stop ambience: fuel island, idling rows at night, the lot.
21. If winter ever cooperates: chains being hung, and chained tires
    at low speed on pavement -- the clatter loop the sonification
    pass wants.

## Paperwork

One line of written or recorded permission covering game use and
redistribution, driver credited (or anonymous, their choice) in
assets/sounds/CREDITS.md like every other source. Offer the credit up
front -- "your truck will be the voice of the game" is part of the
pitch.

## Finding the driver

- Ham radio channels first (Norm is a ham): QRZ and eham forums,
  local club meetings, r/amateurradio -- trucker hams are a real
  community, the ham-to-ham ask carries built-in trust, and a ham's
  cab has a mobile rig in it (real radio texture for the CB layer).
  Some would record to spec themselves for the fun of it.
- The truck stop counter: drivers on a 30-minute break, in person.
- r/Truckers and the TruckersReport forums.
- Trucking YouTubers/TikTokers: they already own quality recordings
  of their own cabs and can license raw audio directly.
- The audiogames.net community: drivers who lost their vision lost
  the career -- this game is for them, and some are already there.

## Etiquette and payment

This is a free, open-source game: the standard deal is voluntary
contribution plus visible credit, and the permission line above is
what actually matters. Floor of decency: buy the meal, offer the
credit as part of the ask. If a driver asks for a modest session fee,
pay it without blinking -- optional, cheap, and their right.

## Ride-along notes

Target OWNER-OPERATORS for ride-alongs: company drivers usually
cannot carry passengers (fleet policy, insurance riders), but an O/O
answers to nobody. Parked yard sessions work with any driver on a
break. A ride-along is worth more than its audio -- hours of in-cab
listening is design research for the realism bar.

Recorder: Zoom Essential series (H6essential) -- 32-bit float means
no gain worry ever, and the spoken accessibility menus mean the whole
rig is operable solo by a blind recordist. Suggested cab setup: XY
capsule at the passenger seat, a lav taped near the shifter for the
engagement clunk, remaining tracks as the day suggests.
